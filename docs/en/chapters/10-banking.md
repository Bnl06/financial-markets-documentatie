# Unit 10 — Commercial and Investment Banking

!!! abstract "Key idea"

    Commercial banking is built around deposits, loans and payments; investment banking is built around capital markets, issuance, trading and advisory services.

## 1. Commercial banking

Commercial banks accept deposits, grant credit, manage payments and earn money through interest margins and fees.

The classic bank was sometimes called the **3/6/3 business**: borrow at 3%, lend at 6% and go to the golf club at 3 o'clock. This image shows how simple banking once seemed, but modern banking is much riskier and more complex.

## 2. Banking book versus trading book

| Book | Meaning | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Banking book | loans and positions intended to be held | credit risk, interest rate risk |
| Trading book | positions held for trading/market making | market risk, liquidity risk |

## 3. Investment banking

Investment banks help corporations and governments with:

- issuing shares or bonds;
- mergers and acquisitions;
- underwriting;
- market making;
- trading;
- structured finance;
- advisory services.

## 4. Market making

A market maker quotes bid and ask prices and provides liquidity. Profit comes from the bid-ask spread, but there is risk because the market maker holds inventory whose value can change.

## 5. Proprietary trading and the Volcker rule

Proprietary trading means trading for the bank's own account. After the crisis, this became controversial because banks could take risks while the system was implicitly protected. The Volcker rule restricts certain forms of proprietary trading.

## 6. Private banking and investment management

Private banking focuses on wealthy clients. Investment management manages portfolios, mutual funds or pension funds.

## Exam focus

You must be able to compare commercial banking and investment banking and explain why combining both activities can increase risks.
