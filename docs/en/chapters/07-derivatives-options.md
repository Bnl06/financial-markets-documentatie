# Unit 7 — Derivatives: Options

!!! abstract "Key idea"

    An option gives a right, not an obligation. The buyer pays a premium; the writer receives the premium but takes on a contingent obligation.

## 1. Call and put

| Option | Right of the buyer | Buyer's expectation |
|---|---|---|
| Call | buy at the strike price | the underlying rises |
| Put | sell at the strike price | the underlying falls |

## 2. Long and short

- **Long option**: you buy the option and pay the premium.
- **Short option / writing an option**: you write the option and receive the premium, but you must deliver or buy if the buyer exercises.

## 3. Payoff and profit

Call payoff:

$$\max(0,S_T-K)$$

Put payoff:

$$\max(0,K-S_T)$$

Profit also takes the premium into account.

## 4. Break-even

For a long call, break-even is:

$$K+premium$$

For a long put, break-even is:

$$K-premium$$

## 5. Protective put and hedging

An investor can buy a put to secure a minimum selling price. This works like insurance: you pay a premium and limit downside risk.

## Exam focus

You must be able to explain why an option is asymmetric. The buyer's loss is limited to the premium, while potential gains can be larger. The writer has the opposite profile.
