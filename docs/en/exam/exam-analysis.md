# Exam analysis

!!! abstract "What is clear from the exam information?"
    The course is assessed through a **written closed-book computer exam**. In the first session, the exam counts for **18/20** and the assignment for **2/20**. In the retake session, the exam counts for **100%**. Excel may be used for small exercises, but **no formula sheet** is provided.

## Exam format

| Element | What this means for you |
|---|---|
| Multiple choice with one correct answer | Recognise definitions and applications precisely. |
| Multiple choice with multiple correct answers | Judge every option separately; do not stop after one correct option. |
| Concept checks | Show briefly that you understand the mechanism. |
| Concept definitions | Define the term exactly and link it to markets/institutions. |
| Small exercises | Apply formulas in Excel or manually, without a cheat sheet. |
| Open/reflective questions | Structure a financial problem and connect concepts. |

## What should you be able to do according to the slides?

- Explain concepts **concisely and correctly**.
- Explain relationships with **financial markets and institutions**.
- Compare financial concepts.
- Outline the role and institutional structure of markets and institutions.
- Reflect on financial problems.
- Solve small exercises.

## Clearly important themes

1. **Financial system:** haves/havenots, direct/indirect finance, primary/secondary market.
2. **Money and bonds:** Fisher relation, simple/compound interest, PV/FV, annuity, bond pricing, YTM.
3. **Money and bond markets:** repo, collateral, T-bills, ratings, issuance, covenants, ABS.
4. **Equity markets:** shareholder rights, orders, indices, active/passive funds, DDM.
5. **Risk and return:** expected return, volatility, portfolio risk, correlation.
6. **Derivatives:** forwards/futures, options, payoff/profit/break-even.
7. **Banking/intermediation:** arranger, syndication, bank balance sheet, shadow banking.
8. **Crisis/regulation:** 2008 crisis, deposit insurance, capital/liquidity requirements, recovery plans.
