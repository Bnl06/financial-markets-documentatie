# Study path for open questions

## Logical order

!!! success "Best order"

    1. **Unit 1**: who are the actors and how does money move through the system?  
    2. **Unit 2 and 3**: interest, bonds, money markets and bond markets.  
    3. **Unit 4**: equity markets and stock exchange structure.  
    4. **Unit 5**: risk, return and diversification.  
    5. **Unit 6 and 7**: derivatives: forwards, futures, swaps and options.  
    6. **Unit 9 and 10**: financial intermediaries, commercial banking, investment banking.  
    7. **Unit 11 and film**: the 2008 crisis.  
    8. **Unit 12**: regulation and supervision.

## Why this order?

The course builds up logically. You can only explain the 2008 crisis properly if you know what banks do, what securitization is, why leverage is dangerous, what liquidity risk is and why governments sometimes rescue banks.

## Priorities if you really have to choose

!!! danger "If time becomes even tighter"

    Focus first on **Unit 1, 2, 3, 9, 10, 11 and 12**. They give you the basis for answering major open questions. Only then study Units 6/7, unless you know derivatives will be heavily tested.

## Repetition technique

Use these four questions for each chapter:

1. What is the definition?
2. Why does this exist in financial markets?
3. What example can I give?
4. Which risks or consequences belong to it?

## Answer structure for open questions

Always use this order:

1. **Definition**: briefly state what the concept means.
2. **Mechanism**: explain how it works.
3. **Example**: give a financial example.
4. **Consequence/risk**: connect it to financial markets or banks.
