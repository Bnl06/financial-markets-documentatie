# Panic — The Untold Story of the 2008 Financial Crisis

!!! abstract "Why is it relevant for the exam?"
    The film is relevant as a case for explaining crisis mechanisms: confidence, bank runs, leverage, securitisation, systemic risk, too big to fail, bail-outs and regulation.

## Viewing questions

- Which institutions were systemically important?
- Why was confidence more important than accounting numbers alone?
- What is the link between securitisation, MBS/CDO/ABS and opaque risk?
- Why did policymakers face a trade-off between bail-out and no bail-out?
- Which lessons lead to stricter capital, liquidity and recovery-plan requirements?

## Model answer for a film question

**Question:** Explain one reason why policymakers feared systemic risk in 2008.

**Answer:** Policymakers feared that the failure of one large interconnected institution could trigger losses and panic elsewhere. Banks and shadow banks were linked through funding markets, securitised products and confidence. Therefore, one failure could create contagion and threaten the payment and credit system.
